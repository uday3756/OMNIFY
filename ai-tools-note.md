# A short note on the AI tools I used

The brief asked for smart use of AI, so I used it the way a small product team actually would: to think faster, design faster, and tell the story better. Not to replace the taste, but to multiply it.

## Claude (Claude Code)
- **Product thinking.** Pressure-tested the core idea: replace filters and forms with one messy input that the AI untangles. Used it to map the parent's real pain points (which class, which schedule, siblings, trial vs membership) into the three required screens.
- **Building the prototype.** The high-fidelity screens are real, hand-built HTML and CSS rather than flat mockups, so they are interactive and screenshot-ready. Claude wrote and refined that code with me.
- **Copy.** Every headline and microcopy line was drafted and tightened here, kept in a warm, parent-friendly voice.

## Veo3
- Generated the brand video: the scroll-scrubbed hero clip and the 30-second demo. All prompts are in `veo3-prompts.md`, written to hold one consistent mint-and-cream look.

## Image generation (Nano Banana / ChatGPT image tools)
- Used to explore hero and activity visuals in the Omnify palette. In this prototype the photo slots use seeded placeholders so the layout reads correctly; final art would be the generated set.

## How it fits together
AI handled the heavy lifting (parsing messy parent input into structured needs, ranking matches, and giving an honest trial-vs-membership call). I handled the judgment: what to show, what to cut, and how it should feel. The result is one calm flow instead of a maze of filters.

## Time
Built within the suggested 4 to 6 hour window.
